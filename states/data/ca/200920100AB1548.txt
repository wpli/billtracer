			March 09, 2009 An act to add Section 33043.5 to the Financial Code, relating to payment instruments. LEGISLATIVE COUNSEL'S DIGESTAB 1548, as introduced, Committee on Banking and Finance.
										Payment instruments.Existing law, the Payment Instruments Law, governs the sale of payment instruments, as defined. Existing law requires a person to obtain a license from the Commissioner of Financial Institutions in order to engage in the business of selling payment instruments in this state.This bill would define the term “authorized delegate,” for purposes of the Payment Instruments Law, to mean a person that a licensee designates to provide money transmission services on behalf of the licensee.Digest Key
