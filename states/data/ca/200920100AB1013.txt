						BlockFebruary 27, 2009 An act to add Section 3021 to the Penal Code, relating to corrections. LEGISLATIVE COUNSEL'S DIGESTAB 1013, as amended, Block.
											  Corrections: veteran inmates.Existing law generally regulates the imprisonment of inmates in state prisons.This bill would require the Department of Corrections and Rehabilitation to conduct an interdisciplinary assessment of new inmates who have combat experience with the United States military, or are on active duty, reserve, or national guard status, and to develop specialized treatment protocols for those inmates.Digest Key

                                NO  Bill TextThe people of the State of California do enact as follows:SECTION 1. Section 3021 is added to the Penal Code, to read:3021. It is the intent of the Legislature to recognize the particular mental and physical and mental factors, including Post-Traumatic Stress Disorders, that impact men and women who have served in the Armed Forces and who are sentenced to state prison.(a) On or before July 1, 2010, the Department of Corrections and Rehabilitation shall conduct an interdisciplinary assessment of new inmates who have combat experience with the United States military, or are on active duty,
				  reserve, or national guard status.(b) The interdisciplinary assessment shall supplement and confirm data collected at any presentencing assessment.(c) During the reception of an inmate specified in subdivision (a), an assessment of the inmate shall be conducted that shall include, but not be limited to, consideration of which of the following:(1) Any history of substance abuse.(2) Criminal activity, including, but not limited to, gang involvement.(3) Education, including, but not limited to, educational attainment, vocational training, and related skills.(4) Family background, including, but not limited to, family structure and relationships.(5) Social functioning, including, but not limited to, employment history, social skills, and religious affiliations and involvement.(6) Medical and mental health status, including, but not limited to, dental issues and physical disabilities or impairments.(7) Symptoms of Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder.(d) The department shall employ an interdisciplinary team to conduct unit management reviews of the assessments and relevant data. In performing those reviews, based upon the needs of the inmate and in accordance with department
				  programs and policies, the teams shall seek input from appropriate professionals, including, but not limited to:(1) Social workers.(2) Correctional counselors.(3) Educational counselors.(4) Vocational counselors.(5) Psychologists.(6) Physician’s assistants or physicians.(7) Mental health counselors.(e) Based upon the data collected at the assessments, the department shall develop specialized treatment protocols for inmates specified in subdivision
				  (a).
